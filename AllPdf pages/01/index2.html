Poshan Vatika: A community-led nutritional garden promoting sustainable agriculture and healthy eating.

Introduction

  • Poshan Vatikas are nutrition gardens developed in schools and community spaces to grow vegetables, fruits, and medicinal plants.
  • The aim is to provide fresh, local, and nutritious food, improve dietary diversity, and enhance health among children and women.
  • Gardens promote self-reliance and ensure better freshness and nutrient retention in food.
  • They also serve educational purposes, teaching gardening, nutrition, and healthy eating habits to children and communities.
  • Evidence shows that Poshan Vatikas improve food security and provide easy access to nutrient-rich foods.

Objectives of Poshan Vatika

  • Provide Fresh & Organic Vegetables/Fruits: Ensure a regular supply of locally grown, chemical-free produce for communities.
  • Combat Malnutrition & Hidden Hunger: Enhance dietary diversity and provide essential micronutrients to reduce hidden hunger.
  • Spread Awareness About Healthy Diets: Educate children and communities about balanced diets and nutritional values of various foods.
  • Encourage Children Towards Gardening: Foster interest in gardening, healthy eating habits, and sustainable agricultural practices among children.

Link with Poshan Abhiyaan

  • Launched by Government of India in 2018: Aimed at eradicating malnutrition in children, women, and adolescent girls.
  • Focus: Addressing under-nutrition, stunting, anaemia, and low birth weight through a holistic approach.
  • Convergence: Integrating various nutrition-related schemes and promoting community involvement.
  • Poshan Vatika's Role: Supports the mission by ensuring the availability of nutritious food through locally grown produce.

  • Vegetables: Spinach, carrot, tomato, beans, cauliflower
  • Fruits: Guava, papaya, banana, lemon
  • Herbs/Medicinal Plants: Tulsi, aloe vera, mint, curry leaves

Benefits of Poshan Vatika

  • Improves Nutrition & Immunity: Poshan Vatikas provide fresh, organic produce rich in vitamins and minerals, strengthening immunity for children and adults alike.
  • Supplies Vitamins, Minerals, & Proteins: Regular consumption of garden produce ensures balanced diets, fulfilling essential nutritional requirements naturally.
  • Reduces Dependency on Market Food: Growing food locally decreases reliance on packaged or store-bought items, fostering self-sufficiency.
  • Promotes Eco-Friendly Habits: Organic gardening encourages sustainable practices, reducing chemical usage and promoting environmental care.
  • Stress Relief & Learning by Doing: Gardening activities provide therapeutic benefits and hands-on learning experiences for all age groups, enhancing mental well-being.

Role of Schools & Community

  • Involve Students in Plantation: Engaging students in planting activities develops responsibility, awareness of nutrition, and a connection with food sources.
  • Use Garden Produce in Mid-Day Meals: Fresh vegetables from Poshan Vatikas enhance the nutritional quality of school meals and promote healthy eating habits among children.
  • Teach Composting & Organic Farming: Schools and communities can educate students on sustainable practices, including composting and organic cultivation, encouraging eco-conscious behavior.
  • Spread Awareness About 'Nutrition First': By organizing campaigns, workshops, and interactive sessions, communities emphasize the importance of nutrition, healthy diets, and well-being.
  • Through these practices, Poshan Vatikas not only improve dietary outcomes but also foster hands-on learning, teamwork, environmental stewardship, and life skills among children and community members.
